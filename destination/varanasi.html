Discover Varanasi

The spiritual heart of India, where the Ganga flows with divine stories

About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is one of the world's oldest continually inhabited cities. Located on the banks of the Ganges, it's a sacred hub for Hindus and a destination steeped in rituals, temples, and ancient culture.

Key Highlights

  • Famous Ganga Aarti ceremonies
  • 80+ historic ghats
  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple
  • Center for classical music
  • Renowned Banarasi street food
  • Rich tradition of Sanskrit and learning

Must-Visit Places

Dashashwamedh Ghat

The most prominent ghat where the grand Ganga Aarti takes place every evening.

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Ganga Aarti

The Ganga Aarti in Varanasi is an evening ritual at Dashashwamedh Ghat, where priests offer fire to the Ganga with chanting and music, creating a mesmerizing spiritual experience.

Manikarnika Ghat

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

November to February – pleasant climate for sightseeing and spiritual experiences.

Average Temperature

Daytime Range: 15°C to 25°C
Nighttime Range: 5°C to 15°C

Pricing

Affordable for most travelers, but prices may rise during festivals like Diwali or Kumbh Mela.
