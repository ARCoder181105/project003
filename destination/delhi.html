About Delhi

Delhi, India's capital territory, is a massive metropolitan area in the country's north. A city where ancient and modern India blend seamlessly. From the Mughal-era Red Fort to the modern Delhi Metro, the city showcases India's journey through time.

Key Highlights

  • 5000+ years of history
  • World-famous street food
  • 3 UNESCO World Heritage Sites
  • Best shopping destinations
  • Most extensive metro network in India
  • 20% of city area covered by parks

Must-Visit Places

Red Fort

This magnificent 17th-century fort built by Shah Jahan represents the peak of Mughal architecture.

View on Map

India Gate

India Gate is a war memorial located in the heart of New Delhi. Built in 1931, it honors the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army during World War I and other conflicts.

View on Map

Akshardham Temple

A stunning modern Hindu temple known for its intricate carvings, grand architecture, and peaceful ambiance. Opened in 2005, it showcases Indian culture, spirituality, and art.

View on Map
